Disaster First Aid Management

Learn essential first aid techniques and preparedness strategies to manage emergencies effectively.

Disaster-Specific Preparedness and First Aid

Earthquake

Before
  • Secure furniture, heavy objects, and appliances.
  • Identify safe spots (under sturdy tables, doorways).
  • Keep an emergency kit ready (water, food, flashlight).
During
  • Indoors: Drop, Cover, and Hold under a sturdy object. Stay away from windows.
  • Outdoors: Move to an open area away from buildings, trees, and power lines.
  • In a Vehicle: Stop safely away from bridges and buildings. Stay inside.
After
  • Expect aftershocks; move to an open, safe location.
  • Check for injuries and apply first aid.
  • Inspect gas, water, and electrical lines for damage.
  • Avoid using elevators and unstable structures.

Flood

Before
  • Identify high ground and evacuation routes.
  • Store emergency supplies (food, water, batteries).
  • Move valuables to upper floors.
  • Keep sandbags ready to block water entry.
During
  • Evacuate immediately if advised.
  • Avoid walking or driving through floodwaters.
  • Turn off gas and electricity if flooding starts.
  • Move to higher floors or rooftops if trapped.
After
  • Avoid contaminated water; it may contain sewage or chemicals.
  • Disinfect everything touched by floodwater.
  • Beware of structural damage before re-entering buildings.
  • Watch out for snakes and other animals in the water.

Fire

Before
  • Install smoke detectors and fire extinguishers.
  • Have an evacuation plan and practice fire drills.
  • Keep flammable materials away from heat sources.
During
  • Small Fire: Use a fire extinguisher or cover with a wet cloth.
  • Large Fire: Crawl low under smoke to escape.
  • If Clothes Catch Fire: STOP, DROP, and ROLL.
  • Avoid Elevators: Use stairs for evacuation.
After
  • Call emergency services before re-entering.
  • Check for injuries and get medical help.
  • Inspect home structure for safety.

Cyclone/Hurricane/Typhoon

Before
  • Secure outdoor items that may fly away.
  • Reinforce windows and doors with shutters.
  • Stock emergency food, water, and batteries.
  • Stay updated with weather forecasts.
During
  • Stay indoors, away from windows.
  • Move to an interior room or basement.
  • Avoid using electrical appliances during the storm.
  • Evacuate if instructed by authorities.
After
  • Watch out for fallen power lines and debris.
  • Boil water before drinking.
  • Contact family and authorities for updates.

Landslide

Before
  • Avoid building houses near steep slopes.
  • Plant trees to reduce soil erosion.
  • Identify evacuation routes in landslide-prone areas.
During
  • Move to higher ground immediately.
  • If driving, avoid hilly roads.
  • Listen for unusual sounds (rumbling, cracking trees).
After
  • Stay away from affected areas to avoid further slides.
  • Check for injuries and assist those in need.
  • Report gas leaks and damaged power lines.

Tsunami

Before
  • Know tsunami warning signs (earthquakes, sudden water retreat).
  • Identify high-ground evacuation areas.
  • Stay informed through radio or official alerts.
During
  • Evacuate immediately if a tsunami warning is issued.
  • Do not wait for official confirmation; move to higher ground.
  • Avoid the beach and coastal areas.
After
  • Stay away from flooded and damaged areas.
  • Be prepared for aftershocks and secondary waves.
  • Do not return to low-lying areas until given clearance.

Drought

Before
  • Conserve water in daily use.
  • Store emergency water supplies.
  • Promote rainwater harvesting and afforestation.
During
  • Avoid excessive water usage.
  • Use drought-resistant crops for agriculture.
  • Seek government aid for water supply and relief.
After
  • Replenish groundwater through conservation methods.
  • Monitor health conditions (dehydration, heat strokes).
  • Plan long-term water management strategies.

Pandemic (COVID-19, Flu, etc.)

Before
  • Maintain hygiene and stock essential medicines.
  • Follow vaccination programs.
  • Develop remote work/study plans.
During
  • Wear masks and maintain social distancing.
  • Stay informed through reliable sources.
  • Seek medical help if symptoms develop.
After
  • Continue hygiene practices to prevent recurrence.
  • Support community recovery programs.
  • Strengthen healthcare infrastructure.

First Aid Kit Essentials

  • Bandages and gauze
  • Antiseptic wipes
  • Adhesive tape
  • Pain relievers
  • Scissors and tweezers
  • Thermometer
